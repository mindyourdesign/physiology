Robert Forster, MD Honored by the American Physiological Society


September 10, 2014

Robert Forster, MD

Dr. Forster is an Emeritus Professor of Physiology at UPenn, a previous Chair of Physiology, and the American Physiological Society's 39th President.

The American Physiological Society's Living History Project was started in 2005. Since then they have interviewed more than 50 of their senior members, providing a cache of collective wisdom and memories.

Recently they approached Dr. Kevin Foskett, current Chair of Physiology, asking him to interview Dr. Robert Forster, and he readily agreed. In this interview you will hear highlights of Dr. Forster's early encouragements toward a medical and academic career. He details his years in college and medical school, all while serving in the military during the "war years". He quips on the lack of medical ethics between scientists at the time, but makes it clear that this practice led to weighty discoveries.

Dr. Forster tells a tale that weaves together some of the Perelman School of Medicine's building namesakes. He describes his most influential discoveries, and the paths that led him and colleagues there, with great detail. His memories of the Department of Physiology are especially interesting! Truly this Living History is an asset to the world of medical research as a whole!
